Research Statement

My research focuses on multimodal data infusion in pattern recognition and intelligent systems, where I aim to blend human-like perception with engineering ingenuity. As a graduate of Computer Engineering and a professional Machine Learning and AI Engineer, I've honed a diverse technical portfolio – ranging from embedded systems and digital signal processing to deep learning, computer vision, and generative AI.

I have engaged in collaborative research – developing ML-based intrusion detection systems for IoT smart grids, bi-LSTM models for keylogger detection, and sentiment analysis of telecom data. These experiences cultivated my technical breadth and deepened my passion for AI-driven problem-solving.

My research ambition centers on multimodal data for pattern recognition in intelligent systems. I'm particularly interested in safety-critical applications where integrative, multimodal sensing strategies can more reliably distinguish dangerous events from innocuous ones, much like the human sensory system.

My research explores several key themes:

  • Fusion of multimodal data for robust event detection
  • Pattern recognition architectures that integrate heterogeneous data
  • Adaptive intelligent systems capable of learning and reacting in real time
  • Applications in safety-critical environments and healthcare

My long-term goal is to design intelligent systems that perceive and respond with the same nuanced understanding that humans bring naturally – ultimately making environments safer and more responsive to human needs.
